## Task 1: Build an enclosure for your DHT11 sensor
Now that your weather station is operational, can you build a prototype enclosure to house it and improve its operation? Remember, a good enclosure (or Stevenson screen) improves the operation of a weather station by: 
1. **Limiting radiation loading**: Typically, enclosures are white to reflect as much radiation as possible and are double-layered to limit radiation absorption by the sensor
2. **Protecting equipment**: Sheltering electronics from rain and harsh conditions
3. **Allowing ventilation**: Slats and louvers allow surrounding air to flow freely past sensor elements, and dissipate any sensor-related heat.

Use the materials provided by Jay (cardboard, tape, scissors, rulers) to build a prototype to hold your DHT11 sensor. Show it off to your friends! Do some googling to learn more about enclosure design.

If time allows, you can test the enclosure outside to compare temperature values between the DHT11 (in the enclosure) versus the thermistor (exposed). What difference did the enclosure make on temperature measurement? Do you need to improve the design?
